Natural Language Processing (NLP) and Named Entity Recognition (NER)

<!--more-->

Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a branch of artificial intelligence that focuses on the interaction between computers and humans through natural language. The objective is to program computers to process and analyze large amounts of natural language data (DataCamp, n.d.).

Natural Language Processing (NLP) stands as a pivotal technology in the realm of artificial intelligence, bridging the gap between human communication and computer understanding. It is a multidisciplinary domain that empowers computers to interpret, analyze, and generate human language, enabling seamless interaction between humans and machines. The significance of NLP is evident in its widespread applications, ranging from automated customer support to real-time language translation (DataCamp, n.d.).

Named Entity Recognition (NER) is a sub-task of information extraction in Natural Language Processing (NLP) that classifies named entities into predefined categories such as person names, organizations, locations, medical codes, time expressions, quantities, monetary values, and more. In the realm of NLP, understanding these entities is crucial for many applications, as they often contain the most significant information in a text (DataCamp, n.d.).
